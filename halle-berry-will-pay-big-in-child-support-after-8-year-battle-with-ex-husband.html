    Halle Berry Will Pay Big In Child Support After 8 Year Battle With Ex-Husband

    The divorce settlement between Halle Berry and Olivier Martinez has reached its conclusion after nearly eight years. As per court documents obtained by PEOPLE, the former has come to an agreement regarding their divorce and custody arrangements for their 10-year old son, Maceo.

    28 Sep 2024, 00:00

    The divorce settlement between Halle Berry and Olivier Martinez has reached its conclusion after nearly eight years. As per court documents obtained by PEOPLE, the former has come to an agreement regarding their divorce and custody arrangements for their 10-year old son, Maceo.

    The exes will share joint legal custody, with Halle agreeing to provide Martinez with $8,000 per month in child support. Additionally, she mut allocate to him "4.3% of any income she receives above $2,000,000" as supplementary support.

    Moreover, the actress must take on financial responsibility for her son's private school tuition, uniforms, and educational materials, along with any extracurricular costs. Halle also agreed to reimburse Martinez for any of these expenditures incurred during the 2023/2024 academic year.

    Halle and Olivier filed for divorce in 2015 following only two years of marriage. The pair have been engaged in a legal battle, primarily over co-parenting, ever since.

    “It is with a heavy heart that we have come to the decision to divorce,” the pair said in a joint statement when announcing their divorce. “We move forward with love and respect for one another and the shared focus of what is best for our son. We wish each other nothing but happiness in life and we hope that you respect our and, most importantly, our children’s privacy as we go through this difficult period.”

    This isn’t the only custody and child support arrangement Halle recently settled. In May, Halle was victorious in a dispute spanning a decade with her former partner, Gabriel Aubry, over their daughter, Nahla, now 15.

    Back in 2014, a judge ruled that the actress was obligated to provide her ex-partner, Gabriel Aubry, with $16,000 in child support for their 15-year-old daughter, Nahla. Nevertheless, Halle decided to contest this decision.

    In 2021, she achieved a minor triumph as the court approved a 50% reduction in her child support obligations, lowering her monthly payments to Gabriel to $8,000. However, alongside this adjustment, Halle was also instructed to allocate 4.3% of any yearly earnings surpassing $1.96 million to him.

    Most recently, the court has reached an accord to establish a maximum limit on this sum, with the cap set at $4.5 million for supplementary yearly child support.

    “Halle shall continue to pay Gabriel 4.3% of any income she receives above $1.95 million, as and for additional child support for Nahla,” court documents obtained by Daily Mail reveal.

    “However, any additional payments of child support pursuant to this Paragraph shall not exceed $109,650 per year, which is the difference between 4.3% of $1.95 million and $4.5 million,” the documents continue. “The parties agree that additional child support over and above $109,650 per year would exceed Nahla's reasonable needs.”
