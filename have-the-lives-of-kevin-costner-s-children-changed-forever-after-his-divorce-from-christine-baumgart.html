    Kevin Costner and his ex-wife Christine Baumgartner ended their marriage after close to two decades and with three kids under the age of 18 between them. For Costner, it was a messy divorce happening all over again. Just like in his first marriage and divorce, the star actor's young children with Baumgartner had to watch their once-stable two-parent home fall apart.

    The Hidden Figures star and fashion designer's divorce proceedings made headlines for several weeks as they went back and forth in court over divorce settlement and child support issues. It is safe to say that when divorces happen, kids are not left out of the drama that could ensue. Even stars like Taylor Swift can attest to the effect of parental divorce. With the dynamics in the couple's union, Kevin Costner and Christine Baumgartner's kids had to adjust to major changes. Here's a look at their new normal after the pair's marriage fell apart.

    What Happened To Kevin Costner And Christine Baumgartner's Children After Their Divorce?

    From prenuptial arrangement issues to child support and legal fees, Kevin Costner and Christine Baumgartner dragged off their messy divorce for several months. Amid all of these, the former couple's teenage children had to adjust to the structure put in place after their parents went back and forth in court. At several points amid their divorce drama, the kids came up regarding their fate in all of these.

    Christine Baumgartner, a fashion designer, filed for divorce in May, citing irreconcilable differences. One month later, she accused Kevin Costner of blindsiding the children by informing them of the divorce without her presence. In court documents obtained by People, the mom-of-three claimed that her ex-husband made a 10-minute call with the children via Zoom to inform them of the state of their marriage.

    She bemoaned the state of things, relaying that she feared that her children would find out about their divorce from an outside source. Due to this fear, Baumgartner noted that she suggested that she and the Yellowstone actor put up a united front to inform the children of their split. She stated, "I am still confused by his motivation to do this via a very short Zoom session, especially since he was planning on being some five days later."

    By July 2023, Kevin Costner filed a motion for Christine Baumgartner to vacate their $145 million Santa Barbara mansion. The handbag designer was instructed by the court to leave the abode by the end of the month. This was in line with the couple's 2004 prenuptial arrangement, which stated that she had to vacate their abode within 30 days of filing for divorce. According to DailyMail, regarding this, she accused her ex-spouse of trying to make her and the children homeless.

A few days before his ex-wife was expected to move out of the 10-acre family house, Kevin Costner and the kids were spotted in Aspen. According to DailyMail, it was the first time they reunited after Baumgartner filed for divorce. The Bodyguard actor picked up his kids at a local airport before they went grocery shopping, and from the whole scenery, it appeared they were at ease.

    During the back and forth over child support payment, Kevin Costner was ordered to pay Baumgartner a temporary $129,755 per month as opposed to her $248,000 request. She would later file for the payment to be increased to $175,057. On September 1, 2023, the embattled couple was issued the final verdict concerning their dramatic child support issue. The Santa Barbara Judge presiding over the case ruled in favor of Costner as a huge slash was implemented on the temporary child support payment initially issued. He was ordered to pay $63,209 per month.

    How Did Kevin Costner And Christine Baumgartner Reach Their Divorce Settlement?

    Kevin Costner's child support battle with Christine Baumgartner came with a lot of back and forth, but their divorce finalization followed almost immediately. This was due to the prenuptial arrangement put in place when they first got married. Per the documents from the decades-long arrangement, Costner was to pay his ex-wife $1 million when she filed for divorce, which he did.

    According to Us Weekly, Christine Baumgartner, however, kicked against it, noting that the money was deposited into an account created in her name without her consent. She alleged that her former husband made the payment, so she could "tap into this money, so he can argue that I've waived my right to challenge the premarital arrangement."

    Another contentious aspect of the former partner's divorce was when Kevin Costner asked Christine Baumgartner to exit their Santa Barbara mansion. According to documents obtained by TMZ, the TV star stated that she refused to leave the abode despite the moving costs he provided. Baumgartner countered him by stating that the cost of rentals in California requires her to take time to find a new place.
