    Paul Kruger is an American football player who played for the Cleveland Browns who has an estimated net worth of $3 million.

    Paul Kruger started his career when he was studying at Timpanogos High School and became a letterman for four years. He was the team captain and hailed as All-America quarterback.

    After serving for two years as an missionary at Latter-Day Saint, he forego his two years in college at University of Utah. Kruger played for the Utah Utes in 2007 as he was chosen as second-team Freshman All-American.

    Then, as a sophomore, he was named as honorable mention All-American by the Sports Illustrated and College Football News. Kruger was selected by the Baltimore Ravens during the second round, 57th overall pick at NFL Draft 2009. He led the team in winning the Super Bowl XLVII against San Francisco 49ers. He left the Ravens and signed a five-year contract with the Cleveland Browns on March 12, 2013.

    Paul Christian Kruger, Jr. was born on February 15, 1986 in Rexburg, Idaho.
