    Celebrity feuds have always been the most captivating storylines to hear about in the news. Whether it's between your favorite rappers or costars on the set of a new movie, feuds between stars will always catch the attention of people. Feuds were never spoken about before, but in the new age of gossip sites such as Deux Moi feuds that were once unknown have come to light. However, when celebrities decide to take their feuds out into the public, it is some of the most captivating information to read about.

    Related: Was Mariah Carey And Nicki Minaj’s ‘American Idol’ Feud Real Or A Publicity Stunt?

    Who could forget the infamous Selena Gomez versus Miley Cyrus beef from the mid-2000s that turned out to be fabricated by the media? The two just dated the same guy (Nick Jonas) when they were teens, which caused a commotion in the media. While some feuds of the past have been created by the media, some were actually real. Whether it was Paris versus Lindsay or Kim Cattrell versus Sarah Jessica Parker, these feuds are still alive and well today. Here is a list of stars who are still beefing today.

    10 Katherine Heigl And Shonda Rhimes

    While Katherine Heigel portrayed her role as Izzie Grey's Anatomy for six seasons, she beefed with the show's creator, Shonda Rhimes. The two already had a tense relationship, but it got even worse when Katherine withdrew her name from Emmy contention in 2008, reported Complex. After Katherine left the show due to a contentious work environment, Shonda responded by saying, "people show you who they are."

    9 Taylor Swift And Kanye West

    The feud that unfolded in front of our eyes at the MTV Music Video Awards in 2009 was between Taylor Swift and Kanye West. After the singer accepted her award for Best Female Video, the rapper rushed to the stage to interrupt her and say declare Beyoncé should have received the award. They squashed the beef momentarily, but it was reignited after Kanye her a "b*itch" in his song "Famous." Taylor went on to write the album Reputation, which was partially based on their ongoing feud.

    8 Paris Hilton And Lindsay Lohan

    During the early 2000s, Paris Hilton and Lindsay Lohan were notorious party girls. Their friendship crumbled as quickly as it began, as they couldn't coexist together while competing for fame. Later on, Lohan told the press Paris had hit her once, which caused the heiress to retaliate by calling Lohan a "pathological liar." Lohan was also rumored to be dating an ex of Paris'. The feud is confirmed to be alive and well after Paris called Lohan "beyond lame and embarrassing" on Watch What Happens Live With Andy Cohen.

    7 Angelina Jolie And Jon Voight

    Angelina Jolie and her father, actor Jon Voight, have been on icy ground since she was a child. He was absent for most of her life, but they were able to maintain a relationship. However, Voight ruined the relationship after he publicized that she and then-husband, Billy Bob Thorton, were trying to adopt during an Oscar pre-party.

    Related: 10 Celebrities Who Have Feuded With Wendy Williams

    Voight even questioned the actress's mental health after her split from Thorton. Voight and Jolie have since reconciled, but after his pro-Trump video went viral, their relationship is up in the air.

    6 Lauren Conrad And Heidi Montag

    At the center of the drama on The Hills back in the early 2000s were Lauren Conrad and Heidi Montag. The girls who were once thick as thieves dissolved their relationship after Lauren found out Heidi began a relationship with her now-husband, Spencer Pratt. Lauren told Heidi not to get involved, but she followed her heart and started dating anyway. This was the beginning of the Lauren versus Heidi and Spencer feud.

    5 Donald Trump And Rosie O’Donnell

    Before Donald Trump became the President of the United States, he was involved in a massive feud with Rosie O'Donnell. In 2006 she referred to him as a "snake-oil salesman" and called him out for going bankrupt. He later went after O'Donnell when she got engaged to Michelle Rounds, calling her "a loser." Trump has also mocked her weight and her return and departure from The View.

    4 Martha Stewart And Gwyneth Paltrow

    Martha Stewart has continuously mocked Gwyneth Paltrow for trying to enter an industry she knows nothing about, referring to her lifestyle products, Goop. She has poked fun at Paltrow's acting skills and even her famous "conscious uncoupling" from Chris Martin with a dessert name. In response to Stewarts' constant attacks, Paltrow created her own dessert called the Jailbird Cake, referring to Stewart's stint in jail in 2014.

    3 Gabrielle Union And Simon Cowell

    During Gabrielle Union's short stint on America's Got Talent in 2019, she was in the presence of racist jokes, saw discrimination based on sexual orientation, and dealt with too much focus on the female judges' physical appearance.

    Related: 'RHONY': Inside Carole Radziwill And Bethenny Frankel’s Feud

    Simon Cowell, who is one of the show's producers on the competition series, failed to do anything about it. Plus, he smoked cigarettes in front of her even though he knew she was allergic.

    2 Kim Cattrall And Sarah Jessica Parker

    Kim Cattrell and Sarah Jessica Parker, who played best friends, Samantha and Carrie, in Sex and The City, are enemies in real life. Their friendship started to crumble after Cattrell refused to be apart of a third Sex and the City movie, reported Good Housekeeping. In 2018, Cattrall then called out SJH for apparently not being very nice to her. Then when SJP offered her condolences to Cattrall after her brother died, Cattrall replying that she did not need her "love or support," nor is she a friend or family.

    1 Vin Diesel And Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson

    After The Rock joined the Fast & The Furious franchise for its fifth film, the cast was happy to have him on board. Unfortunately, after three films, Vin Diesel was not pleased with The Rock's attitude. The two exchanged quite a few heated Instagram posts, with The Rock calling some unnamed people "candy-asses." Luckily, The Rock got his own spin-off film with Jason Statham called Hobbs & Shaw.
