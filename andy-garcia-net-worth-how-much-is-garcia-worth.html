    Filmography and Notable Projects

    Andy Garcia has an extensive filmography that spans over four decades, with numerous notable projects that have showcased his versatility and talent as an actor. From iconic crime dramas to heartwarming romances, Garcia has left an indelible mark on the silver screen. Here are some of his most prominent movies:

    The Untouchables (1987)

    “You wanna know how to get Capone? They pull a knife, you pull a gun. He sends one of yours to the hospital, you send one of his to the morgue!”

    In this acclaimed crime drama directed by Brian De Palma, Garcia delivers a standout performance as George Stone, a skilled and loyal member of Eliot Ness’s (Kevin Costner) team aiming to take down notorious gangster Al Capone (Robert De Niro). The film’s gripping storyline and intense performances solidified Garcia’s status as a rising star in Hollywood.

    The Godfather Part III (1990)

    “Just when I thought I was out, they pull me back in!”

    Garcia’s portrayal of Vincent Mancini, the illegitimate son of Michael Corleone (Al Pacino), in the final installment of the iconic “Godfather” trilogy earned him critical acclaim and recognition. He received an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor for his powerful performance, which showcased his ability to navigate complex and morally ambiguous characters.

    Ocean’s Eleven (2001)

    “You’re either in or you’re out. Right now!”

    In this star-studded heist film directed by Steven Soderbergh, Garcia joins a stellar ensemble cast, including George Clooney, Brad Pitt, and Julia Roberts. He plays Terry Benedict, a ruthless casino owner who falls victim to a group of skilled thieves attempting to rob his establishments. Garcia’s charismatic and commanding presence adds depth to the film’s exciting and stylish narrative.

    When a Man Loves a Woman (1994)

    “When she drinks, she drinks. And when she loves, she loves.”

    Garcia showcases his range as an actor in this poignant romantic drama, portraying Michael Green, a husband grappling with his wife’s alcoholism. The film delves into the complexities of addiction and the impact it has on relationships. Garcia’s nuanced performance captures the emotional turmoil and unwavering love that defines his character’s journey.

    Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again (2018)

    “Fernando, you’re my fire. The one desire. Believe when I say, I want it that way.”

    In this musical sequel, Garcia’s portrayal of Fernando Cienfuegos, a charming and passionate hotel manager, adds a touch of nostalgia and joy to the film. His performance, alongside a talented ensemble cast, brings beloved ABBA songs to life and contributes to the film’s infectious energy.

    Andy Garcia’s filmography is a testament to his talent and versatility as an actor. His memorable performances in a variety of genres have solidified his place in the annals of film history.
