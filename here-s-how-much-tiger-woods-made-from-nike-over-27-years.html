    $40 Million Endorsement Deal (1996)

    Tiger Woods, then a 20-year-old golfing superstar, signed a breakthrough five-year, $40 million endorsement agreement with Nike in 1996.

    This was an unusual amount for such a young player at the time, demonstrating Nike's confidence in Woods' superstar potential.

    The agreement marked the start of a long-term partnership that boosted both Woods as a revolutionary sports celebrity, and Nike Golf as an industry leader.

    With Woods as the face of its brand from head to toe, Nike established the athletic apparel standard in golf while benefiting greatly from Woods' continuous dominance on the course, as he won tournaments and major championships throughout the 1990s and 2000s.

    Nike Made Woods the Face Of Its Golf Line

    When Woods won tournaments while clad in Nike hats, shirts, slacks, and shoes, he ushered in the era of athletic wear in golf.

    $100 Million Over Five More Years (2001)

    Following a remarkable five-year period during which he simultaneously held all four major titles and established himself as the most renowned athlete globally, Tiger Woods inked a historic five-year, $100 million extension of his contract with Nike in 2001.

    At the time, this arrangement broke all previous records for an individual athlete's sponsorship. It took Woods' relationship with Nike to new heights, making him the face of the company and bringing in over $20 million annually at the young age of 25.

    With this innovative new remuneration structure, Nike showed that they are committed to positioning themselves around Woods as the best golfer in the world for years to come, riding his unwavering success and notoriety.

    Another $320 Million Over Eight Years (2013)

    Tiger Woods proved he was still the most significant attraction in golf in 2013 when he inked a $320 million, eight-year contract with Nike.

    At the time, the $40 million annual contract was the most significant endorsement agreement ever for a single athlete. It proved that Woods remained Nike's most influential athlete, in generating news and sales.

    It was widely expected that Woods would play into his latter years with this collaboration.

    Even though injuries further limited Woods' career, Nike's investment paid off when he captured the Masters in 2019 and captured the attention of spectators wearing Nike gear at 43.

    $200 Million Over Ten Years As A Lifetime Contract (2021)

    Nike reaffirmed its dedication to the older – but still massively famous – Tiger Woods in 2021, by signing him to a one-of-a-kind ten-year, $200 million lifetime contract.

    Despite Woods' athletic decline as he approached his mid-40s, Nike sought to ensure that he remained a Nike athlete for the remainder of his career and into retirement.

    This pact meant that for years, Nike would benefit from Woods' iconic status and famous career highlights. At the same time, Woods would be financially secure through the company's $20 million annual salary.

    It was a fitting conclusion to recognize his immense contribution to transforming Nike Golf into one of the sport's most recognized brands since the initial $40 million agreement in 1996.
